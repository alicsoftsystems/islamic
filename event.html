Feb 18, Shab e Miraj, 27 Rajab - 1444 AH

Shab-e-Miraj is considered a significant event in Islamic history and is observed with various forms of worship and remembrance. Muslims may engage in special prayers, recitation of the Qur'an, supplications, and gatherings to learn about the significance of the event. Some Muslims also fast on this day as an optional practice to express gratitude and seek blessings.

Mar 07, Shab e Barat, 15 Shaban - 1444 AH

Muslims engage in additional prayers and supplications during Shab e Barat. They may perform voluntary night prayers, known as Tahajjud or Salat al-Tasbih, and recite the Qur'an. Some also engage in specific prayers for forgiveness, seeking blessings, and remembering deceased loved ones.

Mar 23, 1 Ramadan - 1444 AH

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar and holds significant religious importance for Muslims worldwide. It is a month of fasting, prayer, self-reflection, and increased devotion. Muslims observe daily fasting from dawn to sunset during Ramadan, abstaining from food, drink, smoking, and other physical needs.

Apr 17, Laylat al Qadr, 27 Ramadan 1444 AH.

Laylat al-Qadr, also known as the Night of Decree or the Night of Power, is a significant event in the Islamic faith. It is believed to be the night when the first verses of the Quran were revealed to the Prophet Muhammad by Allah (God). Laylat al-Qadr is considered the holiest night of the year and is highly revered by Muslims.

Apr 21, Eid ul Fitr, 1 Shawwal 1444 AH

On this day, Muslims wake up early, perform their morning prayers, and then gather in mosques or outdoor prayer grounds to offer a special prayer called the Eid prayer. After the prayer, people exchange greetings and embrace each other, saying "Eid Mubarak," which means "Blessed Eid" in Arabic.

Jun 27, Waqf Al Arafa (Hajj), 9-Zulhijjah 1444 AH

During the Day of Arafah, pilgrims spend the entire day in supplication, prayer, and reflection. They seek forgiveness for their sins, make dua (supplications) to Allah, and engage in acts of worship. It is considered a time of great spiritual significance and an opportunity for Muslims to draw closer to Allah.

Jun 28, Eid Ul Adha, 10-Zulhijjah 1444 AH

During Eid ul-Adha, Muslims commemorate the willingness of the prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) to sacrifice his son as an act of obedience to God. According to Islamic tradition, just as Ibrahim was about to sacrifice his son, God provided a ram as a substitute. This event symbolizes faith, submission, and obedience to God.
